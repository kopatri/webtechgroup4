Perfomance Optimization for a sustainable web design

Website performance optimization is something that should be top priority, especially when there is so much online competition. Websites are getting slower, internet uses a colossal amount of electricity and content on the websites is less interesting and efficient then ever.
Web engineers have therefore discussed on the different ways to create sustainable websites where performance optimization is mandatory. This section will thus discuss on the different priorities and the ways to change how web deigners work.

Improve a website's optimization

Website speed optimization

Sustainability and page speed go hand-in-hand. When your website runs more efficiently, one uses less processing power, which means that the wepbpage uses less energy and will have a lower carbon footprint. Customers expect to get the content they want instantaneously, and pages load more quickly, reducing a site's bounce rate and keeping customers engaged and satisfied.

User Interface - ane efficient content

Sustainable design is a design that is efficient and accessible. Creating good experiences for both mobile and desktop users improves accessibility as it makes it easy for people to access your site no matter what hardware they have. Mobile-first web design also helps you avoid loading large assets designed for desktop machines, which improves your site's speed and energy efficiency.

Consequences of non sustainable pages

Internet uses a colossal amount of electricity. In most countries, this electricity is produced by burning of fossil fuels. This, in turn, means that the Internet’s carbon footprint has grown to the point where it may have eclipsed global air travel, and this makes the Internet the largest coal-fired machine on Earth.

Findability

The easier the content is to find, the fewer pages a user has to load to locate information. This means fewer server requests are made and that unnecessary page elements — like photos and videos, which take up a lot of bandwidth — don’t load, either. These small energy savings accumulate over time.

Frontend Optimization

Here are a set of questions a developer should pay attention to when redesigning his website:

  • What deserves or need to be on the homepage?

  • What's the real value of each element of the homepage?

  • Is this element (e.g. nav-bar) here because it has a real purpose, or is it by convetion because a website without a nav-bar doesn't have its place?
